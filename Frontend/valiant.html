Clinical Overview

Bridging the Gap Between Diagnosis and Daily Safety

Alzheimer's disease progressively impairs a patient's ability to recognise faces and identify familiar objects — two cognitive functions foundational to safe, independent daily living. Consequences range from social withdrawal and emotional distress to life-threatening medication errors.

VALIANT is being developed in response to a clear clinical need: an assistive tool that could supplement declining cognitive function in real time, without complex interfaces, wearables, or reliance on constant caregiver presence.

Clinical Rationale VALIANT functions as a passive environmental sensor — it requires no interaction from the patient, operates continuously, and delivers orientation cues through natural audio-visual channels. It is designed to complement, not replace, professional care.
Clinical Challenge

Medication Misidentification

Alzheimer's patients frequently struggle to distinguish medications by appearance alone. Misidentification is a primary contributing factor to medication errors and a leading cause of preventable hospitalisation.

Clinical Challenge

Prosopagnosia & Social Anxiety

Progressive face-recognition impairment is distressing for both patients and families. Failing to recognise a spouse or child is among the most reported quality-of-life concerns in mid-stage Alzheimer's care.

VALIANT Response

Real-Time Identification & Guidance

VALIANT is designed so that its camera system can continuously identify registered persons and known substances, flagging detections for caregivers before distress or error occurs — with the aim of supporting both patient safety and caregiver confidence.

System Protocol

A Three-Stage Clinical Process

VALIANT's detection pipeline is designed around a simple clinical principle: identify relevant people and substances, and surface that information to caregivers promptly and without disruption to routine.

01

Environmental Capture

Fixed or ambient cameras monitor the patient's primary living spaces continuously. The system operates passively — no patient interaction is required. High-contrast imaging with low-light compensation supports reliable detection throughout the day.

02

Biometric & Object Analysis

A multi-model neural network performs facial recognition against registered profiles and classifies substances — medications, household chemicals, food, and hazards — against a curated medical-object database.

03

Detection Alerts

When a person or substance is identified, a visual alert is generated for the patient's environment and caregivers receive a structured notification — detailing what was detected, its category, and when it occurred.
